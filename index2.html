Subtle Frequency Ratios

You might have heard that note frequencies that are an octave apart are related by a ratio of 2:1. In other words, a note that vibrates twice as fast as another is an octave higher. A vibration with a frequency of 220 times per second is sometimes called A3; in turn a frequency of 440 times per second is sometimes called A4.

What about other notes and intervals? If you compared the frequencies of the major third, what ratio would you find?
